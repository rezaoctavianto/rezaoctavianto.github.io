Tourist Destination

Lawang Sewu is a historic Dutch colonial building famous for its distinctive architecture, featuring numerous doors and windows. Originally built as the headquarters for the Dutch East Indies Railway Company in the early 20th century, it now serves as a museum and is known for its eerie reputation as a possibly haunted site.
Kota Lama, or "Old Town," is a historic district known for its well-preserved Dutch colonial architecture. This charming area showcases a blend of European and Javanese architectural influences, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and tourists looking to experience the charm of a bygone era.
Sam Poh Kong is a revered historic temple that holds profound cultural and religious significance. It stands as a symbol of Indonesia's rich heritage and the influence of Chinese culture in the region. This centuries-old temple, with its intricate architecture and serene surroundings, offers a spiritual sanctuary for worshippers and visitors alike.
